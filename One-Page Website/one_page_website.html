    What is a Chimpanzee

    Pan troglodytes or Chimpanzees are a species of great ape originating from the forests and savannahs of Africa. Chimpanzees have four subspecies and are known to have a lifespan of 40 to 45 years in the wild but even longer in captivity. Chimps on average stand around two feet six inches and weigh between 70 to 130 pounds. The current population of chimpanzees in the the wild is between 170,000 to 300,000 and are considered an endangered species due to poaching and disease.
